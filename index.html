    About Me

    My obsession with code began at 14, driven by that sort of dangerous "what happens when I type this?" curiosity. (Geocities survivors, you know what I'm talking about.)

    Life took a few detours between then and now. I raised small humans—incredible for your debugging patience, catastrophic for your sleep schedule. Then I moved across the country and went back to school. Yes, in that order. Not a straightforward story, but that's kind of the point.

    I kept coming back to code through all of it—not in a "it's my passion" LinkedIn way, but in a "I pulled an all-nighter to fix my friend's website and I don't regret it" way. Make of that what you will.

    These days, I'm focused on semantic HTML, modern React, and accessible design. I build products that work for everyone, and I want to do it with a team that values growth and learning as much as I do.

    After all, I'm still that curious kid at heart—just better at fixing what breaks now.
